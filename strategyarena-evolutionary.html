Replicator dynamics
The replicator equation governs how strategy frequencies evolve. The growth rate of strategy i is its fitness fᵢ(x) minus the average fitness f̄(x) of the population.
// Growth rate proportional to fitness advantage
dxᵢ/dt = xᵢ × (fᵢ(x) − f̄(x))

// where
fᵢ(x) = Σⱼ Aᵢⱼ · xⱼ   // fitness of strategy i
f̄(x) = Σᵢ xᵢ · fᵢ(x)  // population avg fitness
Above-average strategies grow at the expense of below-average ones — the system flows toward states where all surviving strategies have equal fitness.
Evolutionary stable strategy
An ESS is a strategy that, once adopted by a population, cannot be invaded by any rare mutant. It corresponds to a stable rest point of the replicator dynamics.
