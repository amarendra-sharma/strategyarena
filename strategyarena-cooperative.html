The Shapley value formula
Player i's Shapley value is the average marginal contribution they bring across all possible orderings of player arrivals.
// Average marginal contribution across all orderings
φᵢ(v) = (1/n!) · Σorderings [v(S∪{i}) − v(S)]

// where S is the set of players who arrive before i
Equivalent closed form: φᵢ(v) = ΣS⊆N\{i} [|S|! · (n−|S|−1)! / n!] · [v(S∪{i}) − v(S)].
The four fairness axioms
1
Efficiency
Total Shapley values sum to v(N) — the grand coalition fully distributes its value.
2
Symmetry
Two players who contribute identically to every coalition receive identical shares.
3
Dummy player
A player who adds no value to any coalition gets nothing.
4
Additivity
For two combined games, the Shapley value of the sum equals the sum of Shapley values.
Shapley (1953) proved these four axioms uniquely identify the formula above. No other allocation rule satisfies all four simultaneously.
Arena
Cooperative Game
You have a set of players, and every possible coalition among them has a value it can generate together. By forming and reshaping coalitions, you explore how the total value gets created and how a fair share for each player is computed. It's a sandbox for seeing when players are better off joining forces than going it alone.
You get two attempts. After finishing your first attempt, check your grade in the app. If you're happy with it, you're done — only use your second attempt if you want to try to improve.
