Why mixing matters

In Rock-Paper-Scissors, no pure strategy is optimal. Whatever you commit to, your opponent can deterministically beat it.

The unique mixed Nash equilibrium is (⅓, ⅓, ⅓). At this point, every action gives the opponent E[U] = 0, so they cannot exploit you.

Key property: at equilibrium, both players are indifferent across all their actions. That's exactly what makes the equilibrium stable.

Mixed Strategy
It's Rock–Paper–Scissors, but the interesting part is the distribution: you set how often you'll play each option, then rounds are drawn from your mix against an opponent with a mix of their own. Some opponents are predictable, some hunt for patterns, and some are unbeatable in the long run. Your task is to choose a mixture that your particular opponent can't exploit.
