Why theory says take at node 1
Theory vs behavior · the gap

In laboratory centipede experiments (McKelvey & Palfrey, 1992), fewer than 5% of players take at node 1, and most games end around node 4–5. Real people pass for longer than SPE predicts — likely because they expect reciprocity, care about fairness, or believe their opponent isn't perfectly rational either. This is one of the clearest demonstrations that pure backward induction often fails to describe actual play, motivating alternative equilibrium concepts like Quantal Response and level-k reasoning.
